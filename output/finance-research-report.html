Product research · 12 July 2026

Make finance a daily operating system, not just a payment form

A repo-grounded review of Dhrubok Portal’s finance module, compared with current education-management products in Bangladesh and internationally.

Scope: one coaching centre, manual/offline collection, Bangla-first bilingual UI, no payment gateway, owners only for finance.

Executive verdict: the accounting core is unusually sound for this product stage. Dhrubok already protects history, allocates payments to charges, supports partials and advances, generates monthly dues idempotently, reconciles balances, produces receipts, and queues SMS. The largest opportunity is not more ledger machinery—it is a faster cashier workflow plus clearer collection control.

1. What exists today

Transaction integrity

Strong

Integer minor units, posted/voided history, reversal of allocations, audit events, owner-only reconciliation, and exactly-once-oriented generation keys.

Billing flexibility

Good

Fee plans, recurring and one-off charge types, discounts, custom charges, configurable due day, partial allocation, and advance credit.

Front-desk usability

Basic

The owner selects one student, manually enters an amount and one allocation, then works through separate blocks. It lacks a unified cashier queue and guided allocation preview.

Collections control

Basic

Overdue preview and bulk SMS exist, but there is no ageing segmentation, reminder history/suppression, promise-to-pay tracking, or collector worklist.

Reporting

Moderate

Daily totals, payment methods, student statements and dues are present. Cash close, collector variance, ageing cohorts, concession analysis, and export workflows are missing.

Local fit

Strong

Cash, bKash, Nagad, bank, cheque and other methods; guardian SMS; bilingual receipts; Asia/Dhaka due logic; and an intentionally manual-payment model.

4. Proposed owner information architecture

Overview

Collection health, alerts, targets, ageing and recent activity.

Collect payment

Search-first cashier workflow with multi-allocation and receipt completion.

Dues & follow-up

Ageing worklist, reminder campaigns, promises and communication history.

Charges & agreements

Fee plans, student arrangements, concessions, waivers and bulk billing.

Payments & adjustments

Posted payments, voids, refunds, credits, references and audit detail.

Reports & closing

Daily register, cash close, methods, ageing, discounts and exports.

5. Delivery roadmap

Make collection excellent

Build the cashier workspace on the existing finance mutations. Add multi-allocation preview, financial summary, explicit confirmation, printable completion state, search shortcuts and focused tests.

Make receivables actionable

Add ageing calculations and indexed reporting, dashboard drill-downs, filtered reminder campaigns, deduplication and delivery outcomes.

Make exceptions explainable

Formalize fee agreements, waivers/credits/refunds, effective dates, reasons, approval and statement rendering.

Make the desk auditable

Add daily cash close, collector identity, drawer variance, exports and reconciliation alerts.

6. Guardrails

Keep these boundaries: no payment gateway unless strategy changes; no teacher finance access; no floating-point storage; no editing/deleting posted transactions; no SMS failure rollback; no full general ledger; no colour-only status; and no finance dashboard metric that cannot drill down to its source records.

7. Suggested success metrics

  • Median time to record a payment and reach receipt: <45 seconds.
  • Payments needing later void/re-entry: <0.5%.
  • Daily close variance: tracked and resolved before the next close.
  • Overdue amount and students by ageing bucket, measured monthly.
  • Reminder delivery rate and collection within 3/7 days of reminder.
  • 100% reconciliation between charge balances, allocations, payments and student summaries.

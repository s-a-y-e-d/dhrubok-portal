A private management system for a single coaching centre. Designed for owners to admit students, organise batches, record attendance, collect payments, follow up on dues, record exam results, and keep guardians informed via SMS.

01

Product Definition

Dhrubok Portal is a private management system for one coaching centre. It is built exclusively for the owners who run the centre — students, guardians, and teachers never log in.

👥
Multiple owners with equal access
🚫
Students, guardians & teachers cannot log in
📂
Teachers exist only as administrative records
🔗
Students may join multiple batches
👨‍👩‍👦
Guardian info lives inside the student profile
💵
Payments collected manually (no gateway)
📱
Confirmations, reminders & results sent by SMS
📝
Exams are offline; portal manages details & results
🌐
Interface & documents support Bangla & English

Courses & Batches

Course

A course describes what is being taught. Examples: HSC 2027 Physics, SSC English, University Admission Mathematics.

📖 Course Fields
Name (Bangla & English) Description Academic session Default duration Default fee structure Active / Archive status

Batch

A batch is the actual student group taking a course. A student has one enrolment record per batch.

📦 Batch Fields
Batch name Course Assigned teacher Start & end dates Weekly routine Room Student capacity opt Default fee Active / Completed / Archive

Batch Page

  • Batch details and routine
  • Student roster
  • Today/upcoming classes
  • Attendance history
  • Fee collection summary
  • Exams and results
  • Add/remove student actions

⚠ Important Rule

Removing a student from a batch ends their enrolment but must not delete historical attendance, payments, or results.

Teacher Records

Teachers have no accounts. Store only administrative information.

Fees & Payments

The financial model distinguishes three things:

Charge / Invoice Money the student owes
Payment Money manually received
Receipt Confirmation of received money

Supported Charges

Admission fee Monthly fee Full course fee Exam fee Material / other fee Custom one-time charge

Discounts

💲 Types
  • Fixed amount discount
  • Percentage discount
🎯 Applies to
  • Admission
  • A particular charge
  • Monthly fees
  • Entire course enrolment

Every discount must have a reason.

Payment Methods

Cash bKash Nagad Bank transfer Cheque Other

Non-cash methods support an optional transaction/reference number. No payment gateway integration.

Partial & Advance Payments

  • Payment lower than charge → charge becomes partially paid
  • Payment exceeds dues → remaining becomes student credit
  • Future charges auto-use available credit
  • Profile shows due and advance balances separately

⚠ Critical Rule

A failed SMS must never reverse or invalidate the payment. It appears in a retry queue.

🧾 Receipt Contents

Corrections

Completed payments cannot be silently edited or deleted. An owner can void a payment by entering a reason. The system reverses allocations, updates balances, and keeps both original and cancellation history.

Publishing

Entering marks does not send messages. The owner must press Publish Results.

1

2
Calculates pass/fail
3

4
Records publication time
5

SMS includes: student name, exam, marks, full marks, and pass/fail. Corrections offer "Save correction and resend SMS".

10

Messaging

Messages are event-based, not a general chat system.

Message Types

Payment confirmation Due reminder Published exam result Custom administrative

Template Variables

{studentName} {guardianName} {batchName} {amountPaid} {remainingDue} {receiptNumber} {examName} {marks} {fullMarks}

Templates support Bangla & English. Each student has a preferred language; owners can override before sending.

Delivery States

Queued Sent to provider Delivered Failed Retrying

Message History

  • Recipient number
  • Message text
  • Related student
  • Related payment/exam
  • Sending owner
  • Delivery status & provider response
  • SMS cost (if available)

⚠ Duplicate Protection

Refreshing the page or retrying an operation must not send the same confirmation twice.

14

Frictionless Interaction Rules

These rules should guide every screen in the application:

Global search is always available
Common actions take no more than 1–2 page transitions
Search accepts partial names and phone-number fragments
Forms remember safe defaults (date, batch, payment method)
Tables provide bulk actions
Desktop screens are keyboard-friendly
Attendance and collection work on mobile
Save buttons remain visible on long forms
Validation appears beside the relevant field
Completed actions show receipt and SMS status
Lists preserve filters when returning from a profile
Printed content has dedicated layouts without navigation
Bangla and English stored separately where exact spelling matters
Financial and published result actions require explicit confirmation
Routine edits don't produce unnecessary confirmation dialogs
